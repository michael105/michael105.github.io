## My "geek side"

It might be obvoius, someone, being in Informatics for three thirds of his life,
also has some propositions best attributed with geeky.

I always did consciously other things: Freeclimbing (Not anymore, but for ten years), studying Philosophy,
Music, and much more.

But, I'm like I am: I experience a real fascination, making things work.
Not only by programming, I also developed and built audio amplifiers, for example.

Here I'd like to point out some of the more geeky interests, related with IT.

#### vi

The best editor available. Ok. It's a bit blown, nowadays.
I already built a micro editor with a vi mode within 600 LOC's, 
maybe I'm going to extend this sometimes, to support all functionality
I'm using with vi.


#### demos

It might have been in 1994, when I got known the first demos.
My PC (a Cyrix M1) was hardly able to play videos.
And I was fascinated by the moving pictures. 
"Second Reality" has been the demo, I've been running over and over.
Since I started programming with gwbasic in 1990, I also realized, 
in how genius the hackers have been, realizing such animations at the quite restricted hardware back then.

#### system programming

I really have fun, applying some tricks, and thinking about how some things can be implemented
the most resource saving way.
I know. And experienced that my own. In most cases, better rely on the compiler.
Even, when you squeeze a few cycles out by some optimizations, this mostly doesn't attribute to the 
code quality at all.
Its another thing with system programming: there, at many places, the code will be used so often, 
even a few bytes and cycles will pay out. 
Thats what I learned out of the development of minilib,
a Ansi-C subset system library.
What at this time is my main project in focus -
It's quite close to have full ANSI-C, and the most used parts of the POSIX C specification.
